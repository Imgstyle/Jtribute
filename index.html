Micheal Jackson, an American singer, songwriter, and dancer.

Here is a timeline of Micheal Jackson's life:

  • 1958- Born in Gary, Indiana, near Chicago.
  • 1978- Jackson moved to New York City to star as the Scarecrow in The Wiz, a musical directed by Sidney Lumet.
  • 1984- President Ronald Reagan gave Jackson an award for his support of alcohol and drug abuse charities,and in recognition of his support for the Ad Council's and the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration's Drunk Driving Prevention campaign.
  • 1980- Micheal Jackson changed his appearance by changing skin color.
  • 1991- Jackson renewed his contract with Sony for $65 million, a record-breaking deal,beating Neil Diamond's renewal contract with Columbia Records.
  • 1991- he released his eighth album, Dangerous, co-produced with Teddy Riley.It was certified seven times platinum in the US, and by 2008 had sold 30 million copies worldwide.
  • 1992- Jackson founded the Heal the World Foundation.
  • 1997- Jackson worked on his tenth solo album, Invincible, which cost $30 million to record.
  • 2009- Jackson announced a series of comeback concerts titled This Is It amid speculation about his finances and health.

Jackson's work from the 1970s to the early 1990s showed "immense originality, adaptability, and ambition" that "generate genius beats, hooks, arrangements, and vocals(though not lyrics)", music that " will stand forever as reproach to the puritanical notion that pop music is slick or shallow and that's the end of it.
